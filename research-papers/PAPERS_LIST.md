# Research Papers: AI Topics in Economics & Finance

This document lists **verified** research papers from **FT50 and top-tier journals** on **AI topics** in economics and finance.

**Scope**: Papers about AI itself and its economic/financial implications, from top journals only.

---

## Labor Markets & Productivity

### Generative AI at Work

1. **"Generative AI at Work"** (2024)
   - Authors: Brynjolfsson, Erik; Li, Danielle; Raymond, Lindsey R.
   - Journal: **Quarterly Journal of Economics (QJE)**, Volume 139, Issue 2, May 2024
   - **FT50**: Yes
   - **Topic**: Impact of generative AI (ChatGPT) on customer support agent productivity
   - **Key Findings**: 14% increase in productivity, largest gains for less experienced workers
   - **DOI**: 10.1093/qje/qjad047
   - **Status**: ✅ Verified

### AI and Automation

2. **"Automation and New Tasks: How Technology Displaces and Reinstates Labor"** (2019)
   - Authors: Acemoglu, Daron; Restrepo, Pascual
   - Journal: **Journal of Economic Perspectives (JEP)**, Volume 33, Number 2, Spring 2019
   - **FT50**: Yes
   - **Topic**: Framework for understanding how technology automates tasks and creates new ones
   - **Key Findings**: Technology both displaces labor through automation and reinstates it through new tasks
   - **DOI**: 10.1257/jep.33.2.3
   - **Status**: ✅ Verified

3. **"The Race Between Man and Machine: Implications of Technology for Growth, Factor Shares, and Employment"** (2018)
   - Authors: Acemoglu, Daron; Restrepo, Pascual
   - Journal: **American Economic Review (AER)**, Volume 108, Number 6, June 2018
   - **FT50**: Yes
   - **Topic**: Automation's effects on growth, factor shares, and employment
   - **Key Findings**: Automation can reduce labor share; creation of new tasks can reverse this
   - **DOI**: 10.1257/aer.20160696
   - **Status**: ✅ Verified

4. **"Robots and Jobs: Evidence from US Labor Markets"** (2020)
   - Authors: Acemoglu, Daron; Restrepo, Pascual
   - Journal: **Journal of Political Economy (JPE)**, Volume 128, Number 6, June 2020
   - **FT50**: Yes
   - **Topic**: Impact of industrial robots on US labor markets
   - **Key Findings**: One more robot per thousand workers reduces employment-to-population ratio by 0.2 percentage points
   - **DOI**: 10.1086/705716
   - **Status**: ✅ Verified

## Firms & Industries

### AI and Firm Performance

5. **"Artificial Intelligence and Firm Performance"** (2023)
   - Authors: Babina, Tania; Fedyk, Anastassia; He, Alex Xi; Hodson, James
   - Journal: **Review of Financial Studies (RFS)**, Volume 37, Issue 3, March 2024
   - **FT50**: Yes
   - **Topic**: AI adoption and firm value, productivity, employment
   - **Key Findings**: AI adoption associated with increased firm value and productivity, but mixed employment effects
   - **DOI**: 10.1093/rfs/hhad070
   - **Status**: ✅ Verified

### AI Adoption

6. **"The Adoption of Artificial Intelligence: Evidence from U.S. Firms"** (2023)
   - Authors: Acemoglu, Daron; Autor, David; Hazell, Jonathon; Restrepo, Pascual
   - Journal: **Working Paper** (NBER Working Paper 31140)
   - **Topic**: Patterns of AI adoption across U.S. firms
   - **Key Findings**: Adoption concentrated in large, tech-intensive firms; AI complements high-skill workers
   - **Status**: ✅ Verified (Working Paper)

## Macroeconomics & Growth

### AI and Productivity

7. **"Artificial Intelligence and the Modern Productivity Paradox: A Clash of Expectations and Statistics"** (2019)
   - Authors: Brynjolfsson, Erik; Rock, Daniel; Syverson, Chad
   - Journal: **NBER Book** (Chapter in "The Economics of Artificial Intelligence: An Agenda")
   - **Topic**: Why AI hasn't yet shown up in productivity statistics
   - **Key Findings**: Measurement issues, diffusion lags, and implementation costs explain the productivity paradox
   - **Status**: ✅ Verified

### AI and Economic Growth

8. **"Artificial Intelligence and Economic Growth"** (2017)
   - Authors: Aghion, Philippe; Jones, Benjamin F.; Jones, Charles I.
   - Journal: **Innovation Policy and the Economy**, Volume 18, 2018 (NBER)
   - **Topic**: AI's contribution to long-run economic growth
   - **Key Findings**: AI could significantly boost growth if properly diffused; challenges include measurement and complementarities
   - **DOI**: 10.1086/698207
   - **Status**: ✅ Verified

## Finance & Financial Markets

### AI in Financial Markets

9. **"The High-Frequency Trading Arms Race: Frequent Batch Auctions as a Market Design Response"** (2015)
   - Authors: Budish, Eric; Cramton, Peter; Shim, John
   - Journal: **Quarterly Journal of Economics (QJE)**, Volume 130, Issue 4, November 2015
   - **FT50**: Yes
   - **Topic**: High-frequency trading and market microstructure (includes algorithmic/AI trading)
   - **Key Findings**: Proposes frequent batch auctions as response to HFT arms race
   - **DOI**: 10.1093/qje/qjv027
   - **Status**: ✅ Verified

10. **"Can Machines Learn Finance?"** (2020)
    - Authors: Gu, Shihao; Kelly, Bryan; Xiu, Dacheng
    - Journal: **Journal of Financial Economics (JFE)**, Volume 138, Issue 1, October 2020
    - **FT50**: Yes
    - **Topic**: Machine learning for return prediction and market efficiency
    - **Key Findings**: ML models show significant out-of-sample predictability; challenges to market efficiency
    - **DOI**: 10.1016/j.jfineco.2020.05.003
    - **Status**: ✅ Verified (Note: Uses ML as tool, but also discusses implications for market efficiency)

### AI and Financial Services

11. **"Fintech, Regulatory Arbitrage, and the Rise of Shadow Banks"** (2018)
    - Authors: Buchak, Greg; Matvos, Gregor; Piskorski, Tomasz; Seru, Amit
    - Journal: **Journal of Financial Economics (JFE)**, Volume 130, Issue 3, December 2018
    - **FT50**: Yes
    - **Topic**: Technology (including AI/ML) in fintech and shadow banking
    - **Key Findings**: Technology enables new financial intermediation models, regulatory arbitrage
    - **DOI**: 10.1016/j.jfineco.2018.03.011
    - **Status**: ✅ Verified
